# Business model notes (internal — docs/ is excluded from the built site)

Owner's plan as of 2026-07-11. Public pages deliberately promise less than this; keep it
that way until each stage is validated.

## Freemium, both sides of the marketplace

- **Senior care facilities:** basic listing (name, contact, care levels, official-record
  links) is free and stays free — that's what makes the directory comprehensive and
  trustworthy. Enhanced profiles (questionnaire-driven detail, photos, room types/layouts,
  richer placement in the experience) become the paid tier once priced. The pitch writes
  itself: better information → better traffic → better-fit inquiries.
- **Placement advisors:** same shape. A great free vetted list is the asset — build its
  credibility first; monetize enhanced advisor profiles/leads later. Don't gate initial
  listing behind payment while the list is still thin; a thin paid list is worthless to
  families and therefore to advisors.

## Early adopters get a durable advantage

Founding-cohort facilities and advisors get the enhanced treatment free (questionnaires,
photos as they arrive). Their advantage compounds: richer profiles rank and convert better,
and they keep what they built. This is the recruiting hook — "free now, grandfathered
richness" — without ever publicly promising future pricing.

## Validation before pricing

1. Recruit the first few dozen facility claims (Oregon first — owner's home turf, deepest
   coverage, Hope Village as the anchor story).
2. Measure the real workload per claimed listing (verification, editing, photo handling).
3. Set the annual fee from measured effort + value, not guesswork.
4. Only then formalize tiers on the public site.

## Guardrails already baked into public copy

- All listing decisions stated as "at our editorial discretion" (facilities apply-page,
  advisor apply-page, vetting standard) — no one is entitled to a listing or its renewal,
  no reason required, without saying so bluntly.
- "Free" is scoped to claiming/basics ("founding cohort" for advisors), never promised for
  enhanced features or forever.
- Never sell ranking. Paid = more depth/visibility of *their own* information, never a
  better position against a competitor on merit pages, and inspection-record links never
  come down. That line is what differentiates us from A Place for Mom-style models and it
  must survive monetization.
