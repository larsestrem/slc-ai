Medicare Five-Star Quality Rating

Who assigns it: the federal Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS), the government agency that certifies nursing homes. What it measures: independent state health inspections, nurse and aide staffing levels, and clinical quality measures, combined into one overall star. Some of the quality data is reported by the facility itself — which means a community that documents every incident honestly can score lower than one that reports less completely. Read the health-inspection detail, not just the summary star.
