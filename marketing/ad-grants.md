If your community is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit — a faith-based home, a nonprofit CCRC, a community-board facility — Google will give you up to **$10,000** per month of search advertising, for free, indefinitely. Not a discount. Not a trial. An in-kind grant that renews every month, showing your community to people in your area who are actively searching for senior care right now.

Microsoft runs a similar program for Bing with roughly **$3,000** per month of value. Between the two, an eligible nonprofit community can have five figures of monthly advertising working for it without spending a dollar on media.

Most never do. Here's why, and what we do about it.

## What the Google Ad Grant actually is

Google Ad Grants gives eligible 501(c)(3) organizations up to **$10,000** per month in Google Search ads. When someone in your city types "assisted living near me" or "memory care [your town]," your community can appear at the top of the results — and Google pays for the click.

The grant comes with real conditions:

- Valid, verified nonprofit status (via Google for Nonprofits)
- A working, substantive website that clearly describes your mission
- Ongoing policy compliance: minimum click-through rates, required account structure, keyword quality rules, geo-targeting requirements

That last bullet is the catch. The grant isn't hard to get so much as it is easy to lose. Accounts that drift below Google's click-through-rate minimums or violate structure rules get suspended — often quietly, with no one at the community noticing until months of free advertising have evaporated.

## Why most nonprofits never capture it

In our experience, eligible communities fall into three groups:

1. **Never applied.** The application involves nonprofit verification, website review, and account setup steps that nobody on staff has time to untangle. It sits on the "someday" list for years. At **$10,000**/month, every year on that list is $120,000 of donated advertising forfeited.
2. **Applied, got approved, got suspended.** Someone set up a few campaigns, they ran for a while, then compliance rules changed or CTR slipped and Google switched the account off. Nobody was watching.
3. **Approved and running, but wasted.** The account is technically alive but spending $400 of its $10,000 on vague keywords that bring the wrong visitors — or no visitors at all.

None of this reflects badly on the communities. You run a care organization, not a search-marketing shop. But it does mean the single largest marketing subsidy available to nonprofit senior living mostly goes unclaimed.

## What we do each month

This isn't a set-it-and-forget-it product, because the grant itself isn't set-and-forget. Every month:

**Keyword strategy built around local, high-intent searches.** We target the searches families type when they're genuinely looking: "assisted living [your city]," "memory care near [neighborhood]," "nursing home [county] reviews." Not broad awareness terms that burn budget on people researching a school paper.

**Ad copy written and tested.** Ads that say what you offer, where you are, and why a family should look closer — refreshed and tested against alternatives so click-through rates stay healthy (which also keeps the account compliant).

**Landing guidance.** Ads only convert if the page they land on answers the family's question. We tell you exactly which pages need what — a clear services list, photos, a phone number above the fold, a simple inquiry form — in plain instructions your web person can follow.

**Compliance upkeep.** We keep the account inside Google's rules: CTR minimums, structure requirements, keyword quality, geo-targeting. This is the unglamorous work that keeps a **$10,000**/month grant alive year after year.

**Monthly plain-English reporting.** One page. How many people searched, how many visited, what they searched for, what we changed, what we're doing next. No dashboard homework.

## The math, made explicit

Your **$2,000** monthly fee buys management of up to **$10,000** per month in donated clicks. If the grant delivers even a modest fraction of its ceiling — and our guarantee floor is **2,000** targeted visits per month by month three — you're paying an effective rate of roughly **$1** per visit.

Compare that to what those visitors would cost anyone else:

| Route | What a targeted visitor costs |
|---|---|
| Ad Grant, managed (this program) | ~**$1**/visit effective (fee ÷ visits; media is donated) |
| Paid search, typical in this niche | **$1–3** per click, plus management |
| Referral platforms | roughly **$3,000–6,000+** per move-in placed |

And these aren't random visitors. Every one of them typed a senior-care search, in your city, this week. That's the most valuable traffic that exists in this industry, and for a 501(c)(3), Google is paying for it.
