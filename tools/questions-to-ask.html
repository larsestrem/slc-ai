Questions to Ask on a Tour

The tour is the community's sales presentation. These are the questions that cut past it - the ones that reveal how the people who live there are actually treated. Tick them off as you go, or and bring it to every visit.

Do your paper homework first, so the tour confirms or contradicts the record rather than replacing it. Read the community's official inspection record and the reviews it has earned - both are linked on its profile in our directory - then bring these questions to see the place for yourself.

The person's daily life

Start here. A community that gets daily life right for your parent is worth more than a beautiful lobby.

Care & staffing

People provide care, not granite countertops. Push for numbers, by shift.

Ask a front-line aide, not the tour guide: "How long have you worked here?" If nobody is past their first year, you have the turnover number without asking management. Ask residents and visiting families: "When you press the button, how long does it take?"

When needs change

Care needs almost always grow. Moving twice is hard on everyone.

How they handle concerns

Every community gets complaints. What matters is what happens next. Process beats promises. (why good communities get complaints)

The real cost & the contract

Get every number in writing, at your parent's assessed care level - never the advertised starting rate. (what's not included)

Before you decide

How to use this list well

You will not get to every question on one visit, and that is fine - the point is to steer the conversation toward the things that matter to the person who will live there, not the chandelier. Watch how a community answers as much as what it answers: a straight number, even an imperfect one, tells you more than a warm non-answer. When you are ready to compare finalists side by side, the comparison worksheet turns these answers into a fair scorecard, and the touring checklist covers what to watch for with your own eyes while you ask.
