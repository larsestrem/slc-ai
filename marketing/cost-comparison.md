Every senior living community buys marketing somewhere - referral platforms, local TV, mailers, a billboard on the highway. The channels differ wildly in one respect that matters more than any other: **what you actually pay for a family who is genuinely looking for care in your city.**

Here is the comparison, with the assumptions on the table. Numbers are typical/approximate ranges, not quotes.

## The channels, side by side

| Channel | Typical cost | What you're paying for | Measurable? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Targeted search ads | ~**$1-3** per targeted visit | A person actively searching "assisted living [your city]" this week | To the visit, inquiry, and tour |
| Referral platforms | ~**$3,000-6,000+** per move-in (roughly one month's rent) | A placement - after competing with other communities inside the platform's inbox | Only at the end; the funnel is theirs |
| Local TV | ~**$10-30** CPM (per thousand viewers) | Broadcast to a mostly wrong-aged, wrong-stage audience | Effectively no |
| Local radio | Similar CPM economics to TV | Same broadcast problem, minus the pictures | Effectively no |
| Direct mail | **$0.75-1.50** per piece, typically sub-**1%** response | Printing and postage to a list, most of whom aren't in-market | Weakly (coupon codes, unique numbers) |
| Billboards | Monthly lease, priced on traffic counts | Drive-by impressions | No |

## What each row really means

**Targeted search ads.** You pay only when someone who typed a senior-care search in your area clicks through to your website. Nobody types "memory care near me" idly. Costs in this niche commonly run **$1-3** per targeted click, every click is logged, and you can trace visits through to inquiry forms and phone calls. (If you're a 501(c)(3), Google will donate up to **$10,000**/month of this traffic - see our [Ad Grant program](/marketing/ad-grants/).)

**Referral platforms.** The big placement services charge communities roughly one month's rent per move-in - **$3,000-6,000+**. To be fair: you pay only on results, which is why cash-strapped communities accept it. But look at what you're buying. The family contacted the platform, not you. Their advisor's inbox contains you and several competitors, and the platform's incentive is to place the family somewhere, not necessarily with you. You've outsourced your inquiry flow - the most valuable asset a community can own - and you rent it back one move-in at a time, at the price of a month's revenue each.

**Local TV and radio.** You pay per thousand viewers or listeners, the overwhelming majority of whom are not seeking senior care for anyone, this year or ever. A $5,000 TV flight might reach 300,000 people to find the few hundred who are in-market - and you'll never know which spots, if any, produced a phone call.

**Direct mail.** At **$0.75-1.50** per piece and sub-**1%** response, a 10,000-piece mailing costs $7,500-15,000 to generate perhaps a few dozen responses of unknown quality. It has a place for event invitations to a warm list; as cold demand generation it's among the most expensive ways to find an in-market family.

**Billboards.** Awareness, maybe. Attribution, never. If your budget is tight enough that every dollar must justify itself, billboards can't make the case because they can't be measured at all.

## A worked example

The percentages below are **illustrative, deliberately conservative assumptions** - not promises. Your actual rates depend on your website, your pricing, your reputation, and your tour experience. The point is the structure of the math, which you can re-run with your own numbers.

Take a modest **$1,500**/month search budget at **$1.50** per targeted visit:

| Step | Illustrative rate | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Targeted visits | $1,500 ÷ $1.50 | **1,000** visits/month |
| Visits → inquiries (calls + forms) | **2%** (illustrative) | **20** inquiries |
| Inquiries → tours | **40%** (illustrative) | **8** tours |
| Tours → move-ins | **25%** (illustrative) | **2** move-ins |

That's roughly **$750** per move-in - against **$3,000-6,000+** for a single referral-platform placement. Even if your funnel performs at half these illustrative rates, one move-in for $1,500 still beats one referral fee. And unlike a referral fee, the spending also built something: a measured funnel you own, a growing base of families who found *you*, and month-over-month data on what's working.

## The occupancy math: why this matters more than it looks

Marketing's real product isn't clicks or even move-ins. It's **demand exceeding capacity** - a waitlist. Two things happen when you get there:

**Vacancy loss disappears.** An empty bed at **$6,000**/month is roughly **$200 per day** of margin gone - silently, every day it sits open. A community that averages two open beds is losing around **$12,000** a month, which puts even an aggressive marketing budget in perspective. Cheap-looking channels that fill beds slowly are expensive; the vacancy meter runs while you wait.

**Admissions can focus on fit.** When the census is tight, there's quiet pressure to say yes to any inquiry. With a waitlist, your team can take the time to make sure your community is genuinely the right setting for each prospective resident - the right level of care, the right culture, the right expectations for the family. Right-fit admissions means happier residents and families, better reviews, easier staffing, and a stronger reputation. That's not selectivity for its own sake; it's the difference between a move-in that becomes a glowing review and one that becomes a difficult six months for everyone, the resident most of all.

Demand is what buys you the room to do admissions well. That flywheel - care quality, happy families, reviews, more demand, better fit, better care - is the actual growth strategy. Marketing just spins it faster. (More on running that flywheel in our [best practices guide](/marketing/best-practices/).)

## The honest summary

- If you need a family who is looking for care **in your city, this week**, targeted search is the only channel that sells exactly that, at ~**$1-3** a visit, fully measured.
- Referral platforms work, but at ~**$3,000-6,000+** per move-in you're renting a funnel you could own for a fraction of the price.
- Broadcast, mail, and billboards make you visible to thousands of people who will never need you, and can't tell you what they produced.

## Direct mail vs. targeted search: the structural difference

With direct mail, you pay three times before anyone responds: you **buy the mailing list**,
you **pay to print**, and you **pay postage** - all spent before you know whether a single
household is actually thinking about senior care this year. Response rates under 1% aren't a
failure of your mailer; they're the math of broadcasting to people who mostly aren't in the
market.

Targeted digital advertising inverts that structure. The ad is shown only to people who meet
your criteria - searching your service, in your area, right now - and you pay only when one
of them clicks. Targeting is never perfect, but well-optimized campaigns paired with a landing
page that matches the search intent position you exactly where the family already wants to
go. The result isn't just cheaper traffic: it's **better-fit inquiries and a higher
conversion rate**, because the person arrived mid-decision instead of mid-dinner.

And that's the whole formula: as long as your tour team can convert families and your care
quality holds up, getting the right people in the door is the piece marketing solves - everything else falls into place from there.
