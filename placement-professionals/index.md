A good local placement advisor can save a family weeks of research — they know the communities,
the pricing, and which buildings are struggling. But most work on referral fees paid by the
communities they place you in, and you deserve to understand that before you rely on one.

**Start here:** [How placement services really work](/guides/placement-professionals/) — the
business model, the questions to ask, and when an advisor is genuinely worth it.

## Directory of local advisors

We're building a state-by-state directory of independent placement professionals — advisors who
work locally, tour with families, and are transparent about how they're paid. Listings will show
each advisor's service area, experience, and disclosure of their fee model.

**Are you a placement professional?** To be considered for a listing, email
**advisors@senior.living.community** with your name, business, service area, years of experience,
and a plain-language description of how you're compensated. Transparency about fees is a
condition of listing.

## Free alternatives

If you'd rather not use a commercial service, real help is available at no cost:

- **Eldercare Locator** — 1-800-677-1116, the federal service connecting families to local aging resources
- **Your Area Agency on Aging** — care navigation and options counseling in every county
- **Long-Term Care Ombudsman** — advocates for residents; can tell you about complaint histories
- [Government help finding and paying for care](/guides/government-assistance-programs/)
