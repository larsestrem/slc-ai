# Profile-upgrade email (ready to send)

One email that asks a facility for everything a family checks — pictures plus the exact
information our how-to-choose guide tells families to look for. Send after a listing is
claimed (or as email 2 in the claim sequence). Reply-to: listings@senior.living.community.

---

**Subject:** {{Community Name}}'s listing — 10 minutes of answers, a much stronger page

Hi {{First Name}},

Families researching senior care in {{City}} are viewing {{Community Name}}'s page on
senior.living.community. Right now it shows the verified basics. The listings families spend
the most time on — and call from — are the ones that answer the questions families actually
bring to a tour. You can get all of that onto your page by replying to this email; ten to
fifteen minutes covers most of it, and partial answers are welcome (send what you have, add
the rest anytime).

**1) Two photos to start**
- The front of your community (a daytime exterior — the view a family sees arriving)
- Your logo
Attach them to your reply. If you have more you're proud of — rooms, dining, activity
spaces, gardens — send those too with a line about what each shows. (Please only send images
you own the rights to, with no identifiable residents unless you hold written consent.)

**2) The questions families check first** (reply inline with short answers)
1. Care levels you offer, and what triggers a move to a higher level or a different building
2. Your starting monthly rate — and what a *typical* resident actually pays once care levels
   are assessed (families trust ranges more than "starting at")
3. What's included in base rent vs. billed separately (care levels/points, medication
   management, supplies, transportation, salon, second-person fee)
4. Caregiving staff on duty days vs. overnight, and your nurse coverage (24/7, daytime, on call)
5. A recent activity calendar (attach the real one — it's the single most persuasive
   document a community can share)
6. Dining: style, meals included, room delivery and its charge, diets you accommodate
7. Pets: allowed? size/type limits, fees
8. Couples: can partners live together at different care levels? second-person pricing?
9. Age requirement, if any
10. Transportation: scheduled shuttle destinations, medical-appointment rides, any fees
11. Do you accept Medicaid — directly, or after a period of private pay?
12. Respite/short stays: available? daily rate?
13. Anything in your state inspection history you'd like context shown for (we link official
    records on every listing — a brief "what happened and what changed" note serves you far
    better than silence)

**3) Optional but powerful**
- Your mission in one or two sentences, in your own words
- Staff longevity you're proud of ("our director of nursing has been here 11 years")
- What makes your community different from the one down the road

That's it. Reply with whatever you have — we'll format everything, and you can correct or
add at any time. There's no charge for any of this.

Warmly,
{{Your name}}
senior.living.community

## Usage notes (internal)

- Questions map 1:1 to the checks in /guides/how-to-choose-a-senior-care-facility/ and the
  facility-page sections (Lifestyle & Services, dining, staffing, ratings context), so
  answers drop straight into the listing fields: care_levels, pets, couples, min_age,
  transportation, medical_services, support_services, photos, description.
- Question 13 is deliberate: it invites the "fixed problem is no longer a problem" note and
  surfaces which operators engage honestly — a signal for the future paid tier and for
  marketing-service fit.
- Track responses in the claim-outreach kit's table; log minutes-per-completed-profile for
  pricing the paid tier.
