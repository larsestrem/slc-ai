This section is written for two readers at once.

If you're a **family**, it shows you what "good" actually looks like, past the nice lobby and
the polished tour, so you know what to ask for, what to expect, and what a genuinely
well-run community does when things get hard. If you **work in or run a community**, it's the
bar, described concretely and centered on residents' quality of life, not on marketing.

We don't grade individual communities here or say who measures up. We describe what excellent
practice looks like and point you to the [official records and reviews](/guides/how-we-list-communities/)
so you can judge any community against it yourself. The through-line is simple, and it's the same
one that runs through the whole site: the resident comes first.

## The practices

- **[Responding to online reviews](/best-practices/responding-to-reviews/)** - what a strong,
  human response looks like, and the privacy line a community must never cross. How a community
  answers a hard review tells you a lot about how it treats people when no one is touring.
- **[Handling complaints and violations](/best-practices/handling-complaints-and-violations/)** -
  every community gets them; what separates a good one is what happens next. The plan of
  correction, the honest conversation, and closing the loop with the family.
- **[Hiring and keeping good caregivers](/best-practices/hiring-and-keeping-good-caregivers/)** -
  the single biggest driver of day-to-day care. Why turnover is the number to ask about, and what
  a community that holds onto its best people actually does.
- **[Resident enrichment and daily life](/best-practices/resident-enrichment-and-daily-life/)** -
  what a real activities program looks like versus a TV in a day room, and why a good day is a
  health issue, not a frill.

## Why we publish this

Families make better decisions when they know what "good" looks like instead of guessing. And
communities improve when the standard is spelled out plainly, in the same words families are
using. Naming the bar helps both sides: it gives families the questions to ask, and it gives the
communities doing this work well a way to show it. See how we think about
[why even good communities get complaints](/guides/why-good-communities-get-complaints/) and
[how to read a community's record fairly](/guides/checking-complaints-and-state-records/).
