Short version: **hold the phone sideways.** Every photo you send us should be landscape —
wider than tall. Here's why we're strict about the one rule that most affects how good your
listing looks.

## Your photos appear in frames we've already built

A photo on this site never floats alone — it renders inside fixed frames designed to make
every community's profile look composed and professional:

- **The profile gallery** — one large landscape frame beside a grid of smaller landscape
  tiles, on desktop and on phones
- **The full-screen view** — when a family taps a photo, it opens wide-format with the
  description below
- **Your directory thumbnail** — the first photo you send becomes a wide, shallow banner on
  your card in city and county listings

All three frames are wider than they are tall, because that's the shape of the scenes
families want to see: a dining room, a garden, a building front. A landscape photo fills
these frames edge-to-edge, exactly as you composed it.

## What happens to a portrait photo

A portrait (vertical) photo dropped into a landscape frame has only two futures, and both
are bad:

- **Cropped:** the frame keeps a horizontal slice from the middle. The ceiling and floor of
  your beautiful dining shot are gone; a photo of your entrance becomes a photo of a door
  handle. You composed the shot — the crop recomposes it without asking you.
- **Letterboxed:** the full photo shrinks to fit, with empty bars on both sides. Your photo
  gets smaller than every neighboring photo, and your profile looks patchy next to a
  community that sent landscape.

Either way, the family sees something worse than what you sent — and worse than the
community one card over. We'd rather tell you the rule than quietly crop your photos.

## Consistency is part of why families trust the directory

Families compare several communities in a sitting. When every profile presents its photos in
the same clean frames — same shapes, same sizes, same quality bar — the comparison is about
your community, not about whose photos happened to fit the template. A consistent directory
reads as carefully maintained, and that trust reflects on every community in it.

## How to get it right in five minutes

1. **Turn the phone sideways** for every shot. That's 90% of the rule.
2. **Stand back a bit** — landscape rewards wider scenes. Show the room, not the corner.
3. **Shoot at eye level, in daylight**, lights on, curtains open.
4. **Lead with your best exterior** — your first photo becomes your directory thumbnail, so
   make it the shot of your entrance you'd put on a brochure.
5. Skip filters, skip stock photos. Authentic beats polished — families can tell.

Full details, including rights and consent rules, are in the
**[photo guidelines](/for-communities/photos/)**. Ready to send? Email your photos with your
community name to **listings@senior.living.community**.
