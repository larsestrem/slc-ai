# Advisor Outreach Kit (Internal)

**Goal:** First 25 vetted placement advisors listed, founding cohort (free). Quotas: **OR 6, WA 6, CA 8, NV 3, ID 2.**

**Ground rules:**
- We recruit against a published 10-point vetting standard - the application at /placement-professionals/apply/ (20 questions) is the gate, not a formality. Better to seat 20 good advisors than 25 mixed.
- Tone toward national platforms: respectful contrast, never trash-talk. Our pitch is what *we* do (transparency, published standard, no pay-for-ranking), not what they allegedly do wrong. The Senate scrutiny of national referral platforms is context we can reference factually, once, without naming-and-shaming.
- Founding cohort is free and we say so plainly, with no bait-and-switch ambiguity: founding members' listing stays free.

---

## (a) Sourcing steps per state

Work states in quota order of ease: OR first (public registry), WA second (association), then CA, NV, ID.

### Oregon (target: 6)
1. Pull the **state registry**: https://ltcr.oregon.gov/Agents - Oregon publicly registers referral agents; this is the ground-truth list. Export/copy all active agents with contact info.
2. Pull the **OSRAA member list** (Oregon Senior Referral Agency Association - find current member roster on osraa.com or via the association directly).
3. **Cross-reference:** advisors appearing on BOTH lists go to the top of the queue - registered with the state *and* self-selected into an ethics-oriented association. That overlap is exactly our vetting posture.
4. Prioritize within the overlap: Portland metro / Willamette Valley (Canby-adjacent service areas) first, solo or small-shop advisors before franchise branches.

### Washington (target: 6)
1. Pull the **ASRP-WA** member list (Association of Senior Referral Professionals of Washington; also appears as NPRA-WA in some materials - check both names, likely the same/successor org). Membership implies signed standards - our shortcut signal in a state without Oregon's public registry.
2. No state registry equivalent, so ASRP membership + business license lookup (WA DOR business search) is the verification pair.
3. Prioritize: Clark County / SW Washington first (Portland media market, closest to our Oregon facility base), then Puget Sound.

### California (target: 8)
No registry, no single dominant association - hardest state, biggest quota. Source in this order:
1. Referrals from accepted OR/WA advisors ("who do you trust in California?") - ask every founding member for one CA name.
2. Advisors who are CPRS/CSA credentialed or belong to national ethics-forward groups (e.g., NPRA national members located in CA).
3. Independent advisors visible in the markets where we have CA listings (match to our facility footprint - outreach is pointless where we have no inventory).
4. Verify: business entity lookup (CA SOS), insurance cert requested at application per our standard.

### Nevada (target: 3)
1. Same playbook as CA at smaller scale: national association members in Las Vegas / Reno, cross-checked against our NV facility coverage.
2. Nevada licenses "referral agencies for residential facilities" - check NV Health Dept licensure lookup for a registered-agency signal before outreach.

### Idaho (target: 2)
1. Smallest market; source via referrals from OR/WA cohort and national association membership, Boise/Treasure Valley focus.
2. Verify via ID SOS business search + insurance cert at application.

**Per-prospect record before first email:** name, business, state(s) served, registry/association evidence found, source, email, one personalization fact (years in business, service area, credential).

## (c) Qualification checklist mirror

Internal screen - mirrors the published 10-point standard so we can pre-qualify before inviting and fast-fail applications without dragging anyone along.

**Fast disqualifiers (any one = decline, politely, same week):**
- [ ] **No fee disclosure sample.** Can't or won't produce the document they give families explaining facility-paid fees. This is the core of the standard; no document, no listing.
- [ ] **No insurance certificate.** No current E&O or liability cert = automatic no.
- [ ] Oregon: not on the ltcr.oregon.gov/Agents registry while actively placing in Oregon (operating outside the state's own registration requirement).
- [ ] Won't disclose which facilities pay them / claims to be "paid by no one" while running a facility-paid model.
- [ ] Application misrepresents credentials or association memberships we can check (OSRAA/ASRP rosters, registries).

**Slow flags (investigate, don't auto-decline):**
- [ ] Less than 1 year in business (allowed, but require an extra reference).
- [ ] Serves families only in markets where we have no facility coverage (park until coverage exists).
- [ ] Complaints findable in state records or BBB - read them; pattern vs. one-off.
- [ ] Exclusive contracts with a single facility chain (conflict with "advisor" framing - needs disclosure language we'd show on the listing).

**Positive signals (queue-jumpers):**
- [ ] Registry × association overlap (OR), ASRP-WA membership (WA)
- [ ] Volunteers their fee disclosure before we ask
- [ ] References from families, not just facilities

## (e) Talking points - OSRAA / ASRP meeting introduction

Ask for 5 minutes on the agenda or a table at the next meeting. Deliver as a peer, not a vendor.

1. **Who I am (15 sec).** "I run senior.living.community from Canby - a directory of 281+ vetted communities in five states. I'm here because I'm adding advisors, and I started with this room on purpose."
2. **Why this room.** "Oregon is the only state with a public referral-agent registry, and this association exists because you chose standards before anyone made you. My advisor directory works the same way - a published 10-point standard, applications, and evidence."
3. **What members get.** "A free founding listing - free permanently for the founding cohort - that tells families a neutral third party verified your registration, your insurance, and your fee disclosure. I show your fee model plainly: facility-paid, roughly a month's rent. Families reward that honesty; the current news cycle around the national platforms is proving it."
4. **What I don't do.** "No pay-for-ranking, ever - not for communities, not for advisors. I link every community's state inspection record and never take it down. The same rules that make facilities trust the directory are the rules that make your listing worth something."
5. **What I'm asking.** "Six Oregon founding spots. The application is 20 questions at senior.living.community/placement-professionals/apply/ - the two documents you need are your fee disclosure sample and your insurance cert, which everyone in this room already has. Come find me, or email listings@senior.living.community."
6. **Posture notes (for me, not spoken):** never criticize A Place for Mom or any competitor by name; if asked about the Senate scrutiny, state facts neutrally and pivot to our standard. If asked "what's the catch," answer the pricing question directly: founding is free forever; a paid tier may exist later for *new* advisors, and founding members are grandfathered.

Same skeleton works for ASRP-WA - swap the registry point for: "Washington doesn't have Oregon's public registry, which makes ASRP membership the strongest public signal a Washington advisor has - that's exactly why I'm sourcing the founding cohort here."
