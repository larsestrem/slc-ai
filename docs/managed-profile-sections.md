# Managed (claimed) profile sections - planning spec

Internal reference only (docs/ is excluded from the site build). Captures the
agreed direction for the extra sections a claimed/managed facility profile
should offer. NOT built yet. These are candidates: some will be jump-nav links
under the H1 (community name), others may just be in-page sections linked from
the body. When we build this, the facility update / claiming forms need to be
updated to collect the fields these sections display.

## Governing principles (these matter more than the exact list)

1. **Render a link/section only when it has real content.** A claimed profile
   won't fill every section; never show an anchor that jumps to an empty section.
   Build the nav from the sections that actually exist on that profile.
2. **Nav order must match on-page section order.** Ordering the sections *is*
   ordering the nav.
3. **Never let managed content bury the objective record.** The official state
   record + reviews section stays prominent and stays in the nav even on a
   polished claimed profile. It is the site's core promise.
4. **Hold the plain, non-promotional voice.** The nav is where marketing drift
   creeps in ("luxury," "resort-style"). Keep labels honest and plain.
5. **Self-reported numbers are not ratings.** Any community-provided stat
   (staffing, resident counts) must be labeled community-reported, dated, and
   paired with a link to the official source. Never present a self-reported
   number where it reads like a verified score. See the no-ratings rule.

## Recommended order and labels

Ordered to match how a family evaluates a place they're already considering.
Group to roughly 7-8 nav anchors; the rest can be in-page sections.

1. **Care levels** - levels of care offered, plus on-site health/nursing,
   med management, therapy, hospice coordination. (Owner prefers the label
   "Care levels." Fold the old "Medical support" and "Services" into here:
   medical/health support under Care levels; non-medical services like
   housekeeping/transport/laundry under Amenities or Daily life.)
2. **Daily life** - activities + dining. ("Resident enrichment" -> "Daily life";
   "enrichment" is operator jargon. Dining is a top satisfaction driver and can
   stand alone if there's enough to say.)
3. **Amenities** - amenities & spaces.
4. **Photos** - was "Gallery"; plainer.
5. **Floor plans & costs** - pair them; families weigh space and price together.
   Keep costs prominent (cost honesty is part of the brand), not buried last.
6. **Our team** - who runs the place; genuinely differentiating, on-brand
   transparency.
7. **How we handle concerns** - the site's signature question. Should be a named
   section AND a nav link.
8. **The record & reviews** - official state record + review links, kept visible.

Plus a persistent **Visit / Contact / Schedule a tour** action styled as a
button, not a section link.

Rationale: lead with the two questions families ask first (can it care for my
parent? what's life like there?), then the practical pair (space + money), then
trust (team, concerns, record), then act.

## Statistics / staffing ratios - the one to handle carefully

Legitimate, top family concern, but it brushes against the no-ratings rule and a
self-reported ratio is easy to present flatteringly. When built:
- Label it plainly as community-reported and **date it**.
- Put it inside **Care levels** rather than giving a self-reported number its own
  headline slot.
- Pair it with a link to the **official** source (CMS Care Compare for nursing
  homes / the state record) so families can verify.
- Never let a self-reported stat read like a verified score.
- Reuse the framing from /guides/staffing-ratios/ ("here's what the number means,
  check the source").

## Additions worth including (ranked)

Essential:
- **How we handle concerns** (signature question)
- **The record & reviews** (integrity anchor; keep in nav even when claimed)
- **Visit / Contact / Schedule a tour** (persistent button)

Strong:
- **Dining** (big satisfaction driver; can stand alone or sit under Daily life)
- **Who we serve / moving in** (care needs accepted, admission requirements,
  waitlist - saves families wasted tours)
- **Location & neighborhood** (proximity to family and hospitals)

Nice-to-have:
- **FAQ**
- **Safety & emergency preparedness** (ties to
  /best-practices/emergency-preparedness/; increasingly asked, esp. wildfire/heat)
- Pet policy, faith/spiritual life, languages spoken - fold into a small
  "good to know" block rather than their own anchors.

## Mobile

8+ anchors under an H1 gets crowded. Use a wrapping/scrolling chip row or a
compact sticky sub-nav, not a long stacked list.
