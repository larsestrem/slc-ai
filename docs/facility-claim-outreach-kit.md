# Facility Claim Outreach Kit (Internal)

**Goal:** First 24 claimed Oregon facility listings. Pace: 5 new facilities contacted per week (~5 weeks of first-touches, claims trailing 2–4 weeks behind).

**Ground rules for this entire sequence:**
- Warm, short, zero pressure. We are telling them something useful about their own listing, not selling.
- No marketing-services pitch anywhere in this sequence. Paid/upgraded anything is a separate, later track that only begins after a facility has claimed and completed onboarding. One line max about that track ever appears here, and it is in this doc, not in the emails.
- Everything we promise (free claim, no pay-for-ranking, inspection links stay up) must match the site word for word.

| 3–5 | 3–5 | Remaining Oregon listings by traffic rank, 5/week | Keep the cadence boring and consistent. |

Selection rules within a wave:
- Prefer facilities where we already display review evidence and an inspection link — Email 2 lands harder when the listing is rich.
- Skip (defer, don't drop) any facility with an open serious enforcement action; we don't want the first conversation to be about their citation.
- One contact per facility. Find the Executive Director or Administrator by name (state license record lists the administrator; cross-check the facility website). Generic info@ only as last resort.

## (c) Phone-call follow-up script

Use 2–4 days after Email 2 for no-response facilities in the Canby radius, or whenever a facility replies with interest but stalls. Call mid-morning (10:00–11:30) — after med pass and breakfast rush, before lunch.

**Opening (gatekeeper):**
"Hi, this is {{Owner name}} — I run a senior living directory based in Canby. {{Facility Name}} is listed on it and I emailed {{Admin name}} about their page. Is {{he/she}} available for two minutes, or is there a better time?"

**Opening (administrator):**
"Hi {{Admin name}}, {{Owner name}} — I sent a couple of emails about {{Facility Name}}'s page on senior.living.community. Not selling anything; the listing exists either way. Two quick things: is anything on the page wrong, and do you want control of it? Claiming is free."

**Then shut up and listen.** Branches:
- *"What page?"* → Offer to email the link while on the phone. Walk them to it. The page itself does the selling.
- *"Everything looks fine."* → "Great. If you claim it, you can keep it that way yourself and add photos. Want me to send the verification steps?"
- *Interested but busy* → "Can I do the five-minute care questionnaire with you right now by voice? I type, you talk." (This is the highest-converting move — do the work for them.)
- *Hostile/annoyed* → "Understood — I'll note no contact. One thing to know: the listing stays up because it's built from public records, but corrections are always free. Have a good one." Log as declined; no further outreach.

**Close, always:** one concrete next step with a date. "I'll email the verification steps now — takes ten minutes whenever you get to it. I'll check back Friday."

## (d) Objection handling

**"Who are you?"**
"A one-person directory based in Canby, Oregon — 281 vetted communities across five states. I'm not a lead broker and not affiliated with any facility or chain. The site's model is public: quality-gated listings, links to state inspection records on every page, and nobody can pay for ranking." (If local: "I'm twenty minutes away — happy to come by.")

**"Is this really free?"**
"Yes. Claiming, corrections, questionnaires, photos — all free, and they stay free. There may eventually be optional paid extras for communities that want more, but the listing and everything I've mentioned in these emails is not that, and ranking will never be for sale at any price."

**"We're on A Place for Mom already."**
"That's fine — this isn't either/or, and it doesn't compete for the same thing. Referral platforms charge you a placement fee per move-in; we charge you nothing and send families directly to you, with your phone number and your state record on the page. Being listed here costs you nothing and takes nothing away from that relationship."

**"Who controls the content?"**
"You control your facts — care levels, descriptions, photos, questionnaire answers. We control the quality layer — review evidence and the inspection-record link stay up and can't be edited or removed, by you or by anyone paying us. That's the trade: you get an accurate page, families get one they can trust, and that trust is why the page sends you inquiries."

**Bonus — "Is this a scam / phishing?"** (expect this on the claim-verification email)
"Fair to check. Verify me independently: the domain is senior.living.community, and the claim process runs through your state license record, so I never ask for anything a stranger shouldn't have. Call me back at the number on the site if you'd rather."

## (f) Success metric: minutes per completed claim

Purpose: this number prices the future paid tier and tells us whether outreach scales past Oregon.

**Log time per facility, every touch:** writing/personalizing emails, calls, verification back-and-forth, entering questionnaire answers, processing photos. Round to 5-minute increments in the "Minutes logged" column. Don't log tooling/infrastructure time — that amortizes.

**Compute after week 5 and again after all 24 claims:**
- `minutes per claim` = total minutes across all contacted facilities ÷ facilities claimed (include time spent on facilities that never claimed — that's real acquisition cost)
- `minutes per complete` = same numerator ÷ facilities at `complete` status

**Targets / thresholds:**
- Under 60 min/claim: the flow is cheap; paid tier prices on value, not our cost.
- 60–120 min/claim: acceptable for founding cohort; identify the two most expensive steps and script or automate them before the next state.
- Over 120 min/claim: stop and fix the funnel (usually verification friction or hunting for contact names) before Wave 3.

Also record, qualitatively: which single step consumed the most minutes. That step is the first candidate for the paid tier's "we do it for you" service.
