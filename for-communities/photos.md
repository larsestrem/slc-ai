Photos help families more than almost anything else on a listing. We're starting simple and
growing from there.

## Start with two images

1. **The front of your community** — a daytime exterior shot of your entrance, the view a
   family sees when they first arrive
2. **Your logo** — the version you'd like shown on your listing

Email them to **listings@senior.living.community** with your community name. If you have other
photos you're proud of, send those too and tell us what they show — we're actively figuring out
which photo types help families most, and what communities want to share is part of that answer.

## Photos we expect to add over time

As listings grow, these are the images families ask for — a useful checklist if you're
planning a photo session:

- Resident rooms: each room type, plus a simple floor-plan/layout image per room type
- Dining room and a typical plated meal
- Activity and common areas (library, game room, theater, salon)
- Exercise/fitness room
- Outdoor seating areas, gardens, courtyards, walking paths
- Activities in progress (see consent rules below)

## Requirements

- **Landscape orientation only — always wider than tall.** Hold the phone sideways. Every
  photo frame on your listing (the profile gallery, the photo page, the thumbnail on
  directory pages) is built landscape, and a portrait photo gets cropped in ways you won't
  like. [Why we require landscape photos →](/for-communities/why-landscape-photos/)
- **The one exception: team headshots are portrait** — taller than wide, roughly 3:4
  (a standard phone photo held upright), shoulders-up, plain background, one person per
  photo. These appear beside each profile on your
  [team page](/for-communities/example-profile/team/); until you send one, a neutral
  placeholder holds the spot. Written consent rules below apply — headshots are identifiable
  people by definition.
- **You must own the rights** to every image you send (you took it, or your community did),
  and by sending it you give us permission to publish it on your listing.
- **No identifiable people without written consent.** For any photo showing a resident, family
  member, or staff member, you confirm you hold their written consent (this is standard in
  senior care marketing — your administrator will know). Photos with no people are always safe.
- JPG or PNG, at least 1200px wide, no heavy filters or stock photography — families can tell,
  and authentic beats polished.
- We may crop or resize for layout, and we'll never alter what a photo shows. We may decline
  to publish any image that doesn't meet these requirements or our
  [content guidelines](/for-communities/content-guidelines/).

**A bonus for your first photo:** the first image you send becomes your community's thumbnail
on directory and city pages — listings with a photo (and a named profile manager) stand out
from the text-only listings around them.
