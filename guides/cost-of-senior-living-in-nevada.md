Nevada sits in an odd middle zone: cheaper than its West Coast neighbors, but now above the national median for most senior care. If you're comparing Las Vegas to Reno - or to a parent's home state - the numbers below give you a fair baseline.

## What senior care costs in Nevada

Statewide medians from the 2024 CareScout (Genworth) Cost of Care Survey. Half of providers charge more, half less, and northern and southern Nevada price very differently:

- **[In-home care](/guides/in-home-care-explained/) (44 hours/week):** homemaker services about $6,864/month ($82,368/year); a home health aide about $7,245/month ($86,944/year)
- **[Assisted living](/guides/assisted-living/):** about $6,110/month ($73,320/year), versus a national median near $5,900
- **[Memory care](/guides/memory-care/):** no official state median exists; using the typical assisted-living-plus-20-30% premium, plan on roughly **$7,300-$7,900/month** (our estimate, not a survey figure)
- **Nursing home, semi-private room:** about $11,209/month ($134,503/year)
- **Nursing home, private room:** about $12,790/month ($153,483/year)

Nevada's nursing home rates run notably above the 2024 national medians (about $9,300 semi-private, $10,600 private). Costs continued climbing nationally in 2025 - assisted living rose 5% to a $6,200/month median - so expect current quotes to top these figures.

## Las Vegas vs. Reno vs. rural Nevada

Here's the pattern families consistently find: **Las Vegas is the value market.** The Vegas valley has a large, competitive supply of communities, and its assisted living and home care rates have historically surveyed below the statewide median - in past Genworth data, Las Vegas home care ran roughly $8-9/hour cheaper than Reno's. **Reno and Carson City price higher**, squeezed by California-adjacent wages and tighter supply. Rural Nevada (Elko, Fallon, Pahrump) has few licensed options at all - price matters less than availability there. Treat any specific metro figure as an estimate and get local quotes.

The insider truth: Nevada is itself a destination for cost arbitrage - many California families move parents to Las Vegas and cut costs 20-30% while staying a one-hour flight away. If your family is already split between the states, run both markets.

> **Ask this:** "What is the all-in monthly rate at my parent's assessed care level - base rent plus every care-level and medication charge - and when was your last rate increase?"

## Will Nevada Medicaid help?

Nevada Medicaid's **Home and Community Based Waiver for the Frail Elderly (FE Waiver)** is the key program. For people 65+ who meet nursing-home level of care and financial limits, it pays for services at home and - through its **Augmented Personal Care** benefit - in licensed residential facilities for groups (Nevada's term covering assisted living homes). Nevada folded its old assisted living waiver into the FE Waiver back in 2014.

Honest caveats: the waiver covers care services only, never room and board; enrollment is capped, so waitlists happen; and relatively few communities participate, concentrated at the lower-priced end. Nursing home care is covered as a regular Medicaid benefit for those who qualify. Rules change - verify current eligibility and participating facilities with Nevada Medicaid or an elder law attorney.

## How to stretch your budget in Nevada

- **Look at residential facilities for groups (small care homes).** Nevada licenses many small homes, especially around Las Vegas, that undercut big-brand communities by $1,000-$2,500/month with better staff ratios. Compare them with larger communities in our [Nevada directory](/directory/nevada/).
- **Shop the whole Vegas valley.** Henderson and Summerlin command premiums; North Las Vegas and older central corridors price lower for comparable licensed care.
- **Northern Nevada families: check Carson Valley and Sparks** rather than central Reno, and compare against Las Vegas if relocation is realistic.
- **Mind the home-care crossover.** At Nevada rates, 44 hours/week of aide care already exceeds median assisted living. Past roughly 35 hours a week of paid help, price residential options too - see [paying for senior care](/guides/paying-for-senior-care/).

> **Ask this:** "Do you participate in the Frail Elderly Waiver's Augmented Personal Care program, and what happens if my parent outlives their savings here?"

## Common questions

**Is assisted living cheaper in Las Vegas than the state median suggests?**
Often, yes - supply is deep and competition is real, especially in older buildings. But new luxury communities in Summerlin or Henderson can run $8,000+. The metro spans the widest price range in the state.

**Why are Nevada nursing homes so expensive when assisted living is near the national median?**
Limited [skilled nursing](/guides/skilled-nursing/) supply and high labor costs push daily rates up. That gap is why Nevada's Medicaid program works hard to keep people in home and community settings.

**Does Medicare cover assisted living in Nevada?**
No - Medicare never covers assisted living or long-term custodial care in any state, only short rehab stays and limited home health. See [Medicaid vs. Medicare](/guides/medicaid-vs-medicare/).

**How solid is the memory care estimate?**
It's an estimate - the survey publishes no memory care median. The 20-30% premium over assisted living is the typical national pattern; always price the actual secured unit.

## Where to get help

Nevada's Aging and Disability Services Division (ADSD) and its Aging and Disability Resource Centers offer free options counseling; the Eldercare Locator is 1-800-677-1116. Nevada SHIP counselors explain Medicare free of charge. For waiver or spend-down strategy, talk to an elder law attorney - rules change, and this page isn't legal or financial advice.
