Families never pay us. Facilities never pay to be listed, and they never pay us to add or correct
the facts on their profile. Nothing you read here about a specific community is for sale. So how do
we keep the lights on? Two ways, both kept deliberately walled off from the directory itself.
Here's the whole picture, plainly.

## What is always free

- Every facility's listing: name, contact, care levels, and the links to its official records.
- The links to the review platforms where families have left reviews.
- Corrections, and any content a community provides about itself: its description, its answers, its photos.

We don't charge a community to appear, to fix its facts, or to describe itself in its own words.
That is on purpose. Because we take no money for any of it, we owe facilities nothing, and we keep
full editorial control. When a community sends us material, we publish what's accurate and leave
out the sales pitch, and if we change more than a light touch, we run it past them before it goes
live. Free content is exactly what lets us stay factual.

## Where the money comes from

Two streams, and neither one touches the information.

### 1. Curated sponsors: senior support organizations, never care facilities

We accept sponsorship from organizations that help older adults and their families but do not run
the care facilities we list: elder-law attorneys, hospice and palliative providers, in-home care
agencies, senior move managers, benefits and financial counselors, medical-alert services,
home-modification specialists, and nonprofits. Every sponsor is vetted for relevance and reputation
and clearly labeled as a sponsor.

We do not run programmatic display ads or ad networks. A fast, uncluttered page is part of what we
offer families, and one relevant, vetted partner is worth more to them than a wall of auto-generated
banners. A senior care facility can never buy a sponsorship here, because that is the one
relationship that could bend the information.

### 2. Marketing services for a few care facilities

Separately, we offer marketing help - filling beds, recruiting caregivers, sourcing enrichment
programs - to a small number of care facilities we think we're a genuine fit for. This is a service
business that lives entirely outside the directory. Buying it changes nothing about a community's
listing: no better ranking, no badge, no friendlier description, no faster corrections, and no
official-record or review link coming down. Any community that has a paid relationship with us
carries a plain disclosure on its profile. The details are on our
[marketing page](/marketing/).

## The wall: what money can and cannot buy

| Money can buy | Money can never buy |
|---|---|
| A labeled sponsorship (senior support organizations only) | A higher ranking or a better position anywhere on the site |
| Off-directory marketing services (a few select facilities) | A more favorable description, or one edited to flatter |
| | Removal of a link to an official inspection or violation record |
| | Faster or preferential handling of corrections |
| | Anything at all about what a facility's record or reviews show |

## Editorial control stays with us

Because we take no payment for facility content, we answer to families, not to facilities. We edit
what a community supplies to keep it factual and to drop marketing spin, and we run substantive
changes past them before posting. We never remove links to official government records, for anyone.
We will remove a review-platform link from a facility's list only in rare, specific cases and only
on request. And if a facility believes a particular review is inaccurate, that is a matter to take
up with the review site that published it: we don't host, adjudicate, or edit the reviews
themselves, we only point you to where they live.

## Why we built it this way

The senior-placement industry runs on referral commissions, which quietly shape who gets
recommended. We took that lever out entirely: no referral fees, no pay-to-rank, no exceptions. Our
income comes from people and services adjacent to the search, never from bending what a family sees
about a specific community. Elder-first only works if the information is incorruptible, so we made
it structurally impossible to buy. If you ever spot something on this site that looks like it
contradicts that, [tell us](/report-a-correction/) - holding us to it is fair game.
