Everything a community publishes on its listing appears under your name, your title, and our
site's reputation — so we hold it to a standard. These guidelines exist to keep every listing
useful to families and fair to every community in the directory. They apply to all
community-provided content: questionnaire answers, descriptions, team profiles, and
[photos](/for-communities/photos/).

**We reserve the right to decline to publish any content or image, and to edit content for
length, clarity, and compliance with these guidelines.** We'll always show you what we plan
to publish before it goes live, and we'll tell you if something was declined and why.

## The standard in one sentence

**Describe what you do, how you do it, and who does it — specifically, honestly, and without
reference to anyone else.**

## What makes listing content strong

- **Specifics beat superlatives.** "Our chef trained at a culinary school and rotates a
  four-week menu with a standing everyday menu" tells a family something. "Amazing food!"
  tells them nothing. Numbers, schedules, names, and concrete details are what families
  remember — and what makes your listing more findable and more credible.
- **Say who, not just what.** "Medication reviews every 90 days, led by our Director of
  Nursing" is stronger than "excellent medication management."
- **Current and honest.** Say what's true today, not what's planned or aspirational. If
  something changes — staffing, services, dining — send us the correction. Honest listings
  age well; inflated ones get contradicted by a family's first tour.

## What we won't publish

- **Details of specific incidents, complaints, or people.** Never describe any particular
  incident, complaint, investigation, resident, family, or current or former employee — not
  even positively, and not even to defend yourself. Describe your *process* in general terms
  instead (see below). This protects residents' and families' privacy and keeps your listing
  out of disputes.
- **Comparisons to other communities.** Nothing that ranks, contrasts, or references other
  facilities — no "the best memory care in the county," "unlike other communities," "highest
  staffing ratio in the area," or naming competitors in any light. Your listing is about
  what you do; every community in the directory gets the same courtesy.
- **Denigration of anyone.** No disparaging other facilities, review platforms, regulators,
  former staff, or families — in any wording, however mild.
- **Unverifiable superlatives and guarantees.** "Award-winning" (unless you name the award
  and year), "the safest," "5-star care," "like family, guaranteed" — we'll ask you to
  replace these with the specific facts behind them.
- **Medical claims.** Nothing that promises health outcomes or presents the community as
  providing medical results ("prevents falls," "slows dementia").
- **Stock photography or images that misrepresent** — photos must show your actual community
  ([and be landscape](/for-communities/why-landscape-photos/)).

## What we ask you to include

- **How you handle complaints — honestly.** Every claimed listing includes your answer to
  "what happens when a family raises a concern": who receives it, how fast you respond, how
  you investigate, how you close the loop with the family, and how complaints change how you
  operate. In general process terms, never about a specific case. Families tell us this is
  the answer they trust most —
  [why this matters more than a spotless record](/guides/why-good-communities-get-complaints/).
- **Who's accountable.** Content is attributed to the person who submitted it, by name and
  title — [we recommend executive staff make the final submission](/for-communities/).

## How review works

1. You submit through the [questionnaires](/for-communities/questionnaire-basics/) or by email.
2. We verify you, then edit for length, clarity, and these guidelines — never changing your
   facts.
3. We send you what we plan to publish. You confirm or correct.
4. It goes live, attributed to you. If we decline something, we'll say why, and you're welcome
   to revise and resubmit.

Questions about whether something fits? Ask first: **listings@senior.living.community** —
we'd rather help you phrase it than decline it.
